# Contributing to MakerCommons

Welcome. There's a place here whether you have five minutes or five weekends.
You don't need permission, a degree, or a startup — just a real problem or the
will to help solve one.

## Three ways to contribute

### 1. Bring a problem 🧩

The lifeblood of the collective. A good problem is:

- **Real** — something you or people you know actually hit.
- **Small & concrete** — "I want an app that does everything" is not a problem;
  "I keep losing track of which subscriptions renew this month" is.
- **Shared** — the more people annoyed by it, the better it fits the commons.
- **Not already a good free tool** — a quick search first saves everyone time.

👉 [Open a *"Bring a problem"* issue.](https://github.com/MakerCommons/.github/issues/new/choose)
Describe the annoyance, who has it, and what "solved" would look like. That's
enough — you don't need to know how to build it.

### 2. Build a tool 💡

Browse [open problems](https://github.com/MakerCommons/.github/issues) and
comment to claim one, or [propose a tool](https://github.com/MakerCommons/.github/issues/new/choose)
directly. Then:

1. Say you're taking it (so we don't double up).
2. Scope it small. Ship something useful fast; iterate in the open.
3. When it's real, we spin up a dedicated repo under the
   [MakerCommons org](https://github.com/MakerCommons) and add it to
   [`TOOLS.md`](TOOLS.md).

**Using AI is encouraged** — that's the point. Use it to move faster and build
more. But you own what you ship: read the code, test it, and be honest about its
limits. "The AI wrote it" is not a substitute for it actually working.

### 3. Help out 📣

Not building today? You can still:

- Improve docs, wording, or translations.
- Triage and reproduce problem reports.
- Review pull requests.
- Share the [Manifesto](MANIFESTO.md) with people who'd care.

## What every tool must be

Non-negotiables for anything that carries the MakerCommons name:

- **Free** — no paywalls, no "premium tier" on the core function.
- **Open source** — released under **AGPL-3.0** (see [LICENSE](LICENSE)).
- **Actually useful** — solves the stated problem for a real person.
- **Honest** — documented, with its limitations stated plainly.
- **Maintainable** — someone else can read it and pick it up.

Optional-but-loved: works offline where possible, easy to self-host, no
unnecessary accounts or tracking.

## Workflow & standards

- Fork or branch, open a pull request, keep changes focused.
- Write commit messages a human can read.
- Be kind in reviews — see the [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).
- Big decisions happen in the open — see [Governance](GOVERNANCE.md).

## First time contributing to open source?

That's genuinely fine and genuinely welcome. Open a problem, or comment on one
that interests you and say you'd like to help. Someone will point you at a good
starting point. Everyone here was new once.
