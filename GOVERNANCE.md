# Governance

MakerCommons is a collective, not a company. Governance is deliberately light —
just enough structure to keep things open, honest, and moving.

## Principles

- **Decisions happen in the open.** If it matters, it's discussed in an issue or
  discussion, not in a DM.
- **Do-ocracy.** The people who show up and do the work make most of the calls
  about that work. Building something beats debating it.
- **The commons over any individual.** No single person owns the collective or
  its output. If a maintainer disappears, the license and the open history mean
  anyone can pick the work back up.

## Roles

- **Contributors** — anyone who brings a problem, writes code, docs, reviews, or
  helps out. That's most people, and it requires nothing but participation.
- **Maintainers** — contributors who've taken ongoing responsibility for a
  specific tool or repo. They review PRs and keep their project healthy.
- **Stewards** — a small group holding org-level admin (repo creation,
  membership, the `.github` repo). Stewards keep the lights on; they are not
  bosses. The role rotates and grows as the collective does.

Anyone who contributes meaningfully and consistently can be invited into a
maintainer or steward role. Ask — it's not a secret club.

## How decisions get made

1. **Small stuff** (a fix, a doc tweak, a feature within a tool): the relevant
   maintainer decides via normal PR review.
2. **Tool-level stuff** (direction of a project, breaking changes): discussed in
   that repo's issues; the maintainers decide, favoring rough consensus.
3. **Collective-level stuff** (the manifesto, the license, adding/retiring a
   tool, changing governance): opened as an issue in the `.github` repo, left
   open long enough for people to weigh in, and decided by stewards with rough
   consensus. Changes to core values (free, open, copyleft) require a very
   strong reason and broad agreement.

## Non-negotiables

Some things are the identity of the collective and are not up for casual change:

- Everything we ship is **free** and **open source** under a copyleft license.
- No rent extraction on core functionality.
- AI is a tool we use openly, never a smokescreen for a paywall.

These can only change through a collective-level decision with overwhelming
support — and if they ever did, it would no longer really be MakerCommons.

## Money

If the collective ever handles money (donations, hosting costs, bounties), it
will be **transparent by default**: what came in, what went out, publicly. No
tool's core function is ever put behind a payment.
