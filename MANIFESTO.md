# The MakerCommons Manifesto

## Why we exist

Software has never been cheaper to build — and somehow, ordinary people have
never paid more for less.

A generation of "AI-powered" products ships thin wrappers around a language
model, slaps a subscription on it, and charges rent for problems that could be
solved once and given away forever. Meanwhile the small, real, everyday
annoyances that actually make people's lives harder go unaddressed, because
nobody can build a recurring-revenue empire on top of them.

We think that's backwards.

**MakerCommons is a collective of builders who use modern tools — including AI —
to solve real problems for real people, and give the result away for free and
open source.**

## What we believe

1. **Useful beats impressive.** A tiny tool that saves one person ten minutes a
   week is worth more than a slick landing page selling a promise. We optimize
   for usefulness, not for demos.

2. **AI is a tool, not a business model.** Used well, AI lets a handful of
   people ship what used to take a team. We use it to build *more* useful things
   *faster* and give them away — not to disguise a paywall.

3. **Free and open, or it doesn't count.** Everything we build is open source
   under a copyleft license. If someone hosts it, improves it, or forks it, the
   improvements come back to the commons. Nobody gets to quietly close what the
   community built.

4. **The problems come from people, not from a roadmap.** Anyone can bring a
   real-world annoyance. The best problems are small, concrete, and shared by
   many. We'd rather fix a hundred small real things than one imaginary big one.

5. **Craft matters.** "Free" is not an excuse for junk. We build things we'd be
   proud to use ourselves — maintained, documented, honest about their limits.

6. **No gatekeeping.** You don't need a CS degree, a startup, or permission.
   You need a real problem or the willingness to help solve one.

## What we are not

- We are not a startup. There is no exit, no equity, no growth target.
- We are not anti-money — makers deserve to be paid. We are against **rent
  extraction**: charging recurring fees for problems that should be solved once
  and shared.
- We are not anti-AI. We are against **AI as a smokescreen** for shipping
  low-effort products at high-effort prices.

## How it works, in one breath

Someone brings a real problem → the collective picks it up → we build a small,
open, genuinely useful tool → we give it away → the next person improves it.

That's the whole thing. Read [CONTRIBUTING](CONTRIBUTING.md) to jump in.
